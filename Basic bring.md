# Basic Brine

A simple 6% brine, suitable for poultry, pork chops, and most cuts you'd brine before cooking.

## Ingredients

- 1 litre water (cold or room temperature)
- 60g salt (table or fine sea salt)

### Optional aromatics

- 30g sugar
- 1 tbsp peppercorns
- 2-3 bay leaves
- 2-3 garlic cloves, crushed
- Citrus peel (lemon or orange)

## Method

1. If using only salt and water, simply stir the salt into cold water until fully dissolved. No heat required.
2. If adding aromatics or sugar, bring a portion of the water (e.g. 250ml) to a simmer with the salt, sugar, and aromatics until dissolved, then combine with the remaining cold water to bring it back to room temperature or colder.
3. **Cool the brine fully before use** — it must be at fridge temperature (below 5°C) before submerging any meat. Never put meat into warm brine.
4. Submerge the meat fully in the brine, weighing it down if necessary.
5. Brine in the fridge for the appropriate time (see notes below).
6. Remove, rinse briefly, and pat dry before cooking.

## Notes

- 6% salt concentration (60g per litre) is a solid standard strength for most poultry and pork.
- Brining times vary by cut: chicken breasts 30-60 min, whole chicken 4-12 hours, pork chops 1-4 hours, turkey (whole) 12-24 hours.
- Don't over-brine — beyond the recommended time the texture can turn spongy or overly salty.
- Scale the recipe up by litre as needed (e.g. 2L water : 120g salt) to fully submerge larger cuts.
