# Kamado Pork Spare Ribs

**Setup:** Kamado with heat deflector, indirect heat
**Serves:** 2-3
**Cook time:** ~5 hours

## Ingredients

### Ribs
- 1 rack pork spare ribs (approx. 1.2-1.5kg), membrane removed from the bone side

### Dry Rub
- 2 tbsp brown sugar
- 1 tbsp smoked paprika
- 1 tsp garlic powder
- 1 tsp onion powder
- 1 tsp black pepper
- 1 tsp sea salt
- 1/2 tsp cayenne (optional)

### Spritz
- 100ml apple juice
- 50ml cider vinegar

### Glaze (optional, last 30 minutes)
- 4 tbsp BBQ sauce of choice
- 1 tbsp honey

## Method

1. Remove the membrane from the underside of the ribs — grip with kitchen roll and pull in one motion.
2. Coat ribs in a thin layer of oil or yellow mustard, then apply rub on both sides. Leave 30 minutes at room temperature, or overnight in the fridge for better penetration.
3. Set up kamado with heat deflector in place. Bring to a steady **110-120°C**.
4. Place ribs bone-side down, indirect. Close the lid.
5. After 90 minutes, start spritzing with the apple juice and vinegar mix every 45 minutes.
6. At **3 hours**, check for the bend test — ribs should crack slightly when lifted from one end with tongs. If not there yet, keep going.
7. Once ribs bend and internal temp reads **88-92°C**, wrap in foil with a splash of the spritz liquid. Rest in the foil (still on the kamado or in a cooler) for 30-45 minutes.
8. If glazing: unwrap, brush with BBQ sauce and honey mix, return to kamado open for 10-15 minutes to tack up.
9. Rest 10 minutes, slice between the bones, serve.

## Notes
- Total cook time will vary — go by temperature and the bend test, not the clock.
- If your kamado runs hotter than 120°C, that's fine up to 130°C; just check earlier.
