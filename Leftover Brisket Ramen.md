# Leftover Brisket Ramen

*A decent use of leftover brisket. Not remotely authentic, but it works. Serves 2.*

---

## Ingredients

| Ingredient | Amount |
|---|---|
| Leftover brisket, cubed | 300g |
| Carrot, sliced into coins or batons | 1 |
| Onion, roughly chopped | 1 |
| Garlic cloves, sliced | 3 |
| Red chillies (or to taste) | 2 |
| Ramen noodles | 180g |
| Water or beef stock | 800ml |
| Soy sauce | 2 tbsp |
| Neutral oil (vegetable or sunflower) | 1 tbsp |

---

## Method

1. **Fry the aromatics** — Heat the oil in a large pan or wok over medium-high heat. Add the onion, carrot, garlic, and chillies. Fry for 5–7 minutes until softened and starting to colour.
2. **Add the brisket** — Add the cubed brisket and stir through. Fry for another 2–3 minutes to get a bit of colour on the meat.
3. **Add liquid** — Pour in the stock. Add the soy sauce. Bring to a boil then reduce to a steady simmer. If you saved the resting juices from the brisket cook, add them here — they're essentially free flavour and will make a noticeably better broth.
4. **Cook the noodles** — Add the ramen noodles directly to the broth and cook according to packet instructions, usually 3–4 minutes. Don't overcook them.
5. **Serve** — Ladle into bowls making sure you get a decent amount of broth. Top with a soft-boiled egg, spring onions, or a drizzle of sesame oil if you have them.

---

## Notes

- Stock is better than water — even a good beef stock cube beats plain water. The brisket resting juices are better still, so save them when you wrap for the rest.
- Soy sauce is the minimum seasoning; a spoon of miso paste stirred in at the end adds depth.
- Adjust chilli to taste.
