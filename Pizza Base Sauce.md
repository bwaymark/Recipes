# Pizza Base Sauce

## Ingredients

- 1 tin (400g) crushed tomatoes 
- 3–4 cloves of garlic, crushed
- 2 tbsp olive oil
- ½ tsp dried oregano
- ½ tsp ground cumin
- ½ tsp paprika
- Salt to taste
- Pepper

## Method

1. Heat the olive oil in a saucepan over a low heat. Add the crushed garlic and sweat for 1–2 minutes until softened and translucent. **Do not let it brown** — burnt garlic is bitter and will ruin the sauce.
2. Add the cumin and paprika and stir for 30 seconds to bloom the spices in the oil.
3. Pour in the crushed tomatoes. Add the oregano, a pinch of sugar, and season with salt.
4. Simmer on a low heat for 15–20 minutes until the sauce thickens slightly.
5. Blitz with a stick blender for a smooth sauce, or leave chunky — your call.

## Notes

**Sweating garlic** means cooking it gently in oil over a low heat until it softens and turns translucent, without colouring it. The aim is to mellow the raw, sharp flavour. If it starts to turn golden, your heat is too high — take it off or turn it down immediately.

The cumin and paprika give this a slightly warmer, smokier profile than a straight Neapolitan base — works particularly well with meat toppings.
