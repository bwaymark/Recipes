# Pork Souvlaki & Tzatziki

---

## Pork Souvlaki

*Greek grilled pork skewers. Works well on the kettle over direct heat. Serves 4.*

### Ingredients

- 800g pork shoulder (preferred) or pork loin, cut into 3cm cubes (4cm if using loin)
- 4 tbsp olive oil
- 1 lemon, juiced
- 3 garlic cloves, minced
- 2 tsp dried oregano
- 1 tsp dried thyme
- 1.5 tsp salt
- 0.5 tsp black pepper
- 1 tsp sweet paprika

### Method

1. **Make the marinade** — Mix together the olive oil, lemon juice, garlic, oregano, thyme, salt, pepper, and paprika in a bowl large enough to hold the pork.
2. **Marinate the pork** — Add the pork and toss well to coat. Cover and refrigerate for at least 2 hours — overnight is better. If using loin, go the full overnight; it needs the time more than shoulder does.
3. **Prepare skewers** — If using wooden skewers, soak them in water for 30 minutes beforehand. Thread the pork onto skewers, packing the pieces fairly snugly.
4. **Set up the kettle** — Light a full chimney of charcoal — around 80–90 briquettes. Once ashed over, split evenly between your two charcoal baskets and push them to either side of the grill. This gives you a hot direct zone over the coals and a cooler middle zone with no heat directly underneath. Target grill temperature: 220–240°C with the lid on.
5. **Grill the skewers** — Place skewers over the coals. Grill for 8–12 minutes total, turning every 2–3 minutes, until charred at the edges and cooked through. If they're colouring too fast before the centre is done, slide them to the middle zone and put the lid back on. Internal temperature should reach 70°C for shoulder, or 65°C for loin — loin goes from done to dry quickly, so pull it earlier.
6. **Rest and serve** — Rest for 5 minutes before serving. Serve in warm flatbreads with tzatziki, sliced tomato, red onion, and a squeeze of lemon.

> **Notes:** Shoulder is the better cut — more forgiving and more flavour. Loin works but is less tolerant of heat; cut it larger, pull it earlier, and don't let it sit on direct heat too long. Chicken thighs work well with the same marinade. Keep the lid down between turns to maintain heat. The kettle runs less predictably than the kamado so watch them from around the 8-minute mark.

## Tzatziki

*The proper accompaniment. Takes about 10 minutes. Serves 4.*

### Ingredients

- 500g full-fat Greek yogurt
- 1 cucumber
- 2 garlic cloves, minced
- 2 tbsp olive oil
- 1 lemon, juiced
- 1 tbsp fresh dill, finely chopped
- 0.5 tsp salt
- 0.25 tsp black pepper

### Method

1. **Prepare the cucumber** — Grate the cucumber coarsely. Wrap in a clean tea towel and squeeze out as much water as possible. Don't skip this — you'll get watery tzatziki if you do.
2. **Mix everything together** — Combine the yogurt, squeezed cucumber, garlic, olive oil, lemon juice, dill, salt, and pepper. Mix well.
3. **Rest in the fridge** — Cover and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes before serving. The flavours improve noticeably with a bit of time.

> **Notes:** Full-fat Greek yogurt only — low-fat versions are too thin and taste of nothing. Fresh dill is traditional; mint works too. Keeps in the fridge for 2–3 days.
