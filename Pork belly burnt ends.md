Ingredients

	•	Pork belly, cut into 3–4cm cubes (skin removed)
	•	Your favourite pork rub, for dusting
	•	4 tbsp runny honey
	•	Your favourite BBQ sauce

Method

	1.	Remove the skin from the pork belly, then cut into 3–4cm cubes.
	2.	Dust the cubes with your rub in a bowl until evenly coated.
	3.	Set up your kamado for an indirect cook — plate setter in, stainless steel grid on top — and bring it to 110°C. Allow it to stabilise. Place a small foil tray with water or apple juice on the plate setter to add humidity to the cooking chamber.
	4.	Add a couple of wood chunks to the burning charcoal before replacing the grid.
	5.	Place the pork belly cubes on the grid, leaving space between each piece.
	6.	Cook for approximately 2–2.5 hours until they reach an internal temperature of 80°C or feel "probe tender."
	7.	Transfer the cubes to a pan, add the honey, BBQ sauce, and a splash of apple juice (or apple cider vinegar/stock). Stir to coat.
	8.	Cover the pan tightly with foil and return to the kamado for 1 hour. Remove the foil for the final 30 minutes to allow the sauce to thicken and glaze the meat. Keep an eye on it — burnt honey is not the same as burnt ends.
