# Low & Slow Pulled Lamb Leg — Kamado

## Overview
- **Cut:** 1kg bone-in lamb leg (or half leg)
- **Cook time:** 10 hours
- **Pit temp:** 130°C
- **Pull temp:** 93–95°C internal
- **Rest:** 45 minutes 

---

## Rub

| Ingredient | Amount |
|---|---|
| Flaked sea salt | 10g |
| Black pepper, coarse ground | 5g |
| Cumin, ground | 4g |
| Coriander, ground | 3g |
| Smoked paprika | 4g |
| Garlic powder | 3g |
| Dried oregano | 2g |
| Cayenne | 1g |

Mix together and apply generously. For best results, rub the night before and leave uncovered in the fridge overnight.

---

## Mop / Spritz

Mix together in a spray bottle:

| Ingredient | Amount |
|---|---|
| Red wine vinegar | 50ml |
| Olive oil | 25ml |
| Water | 50ml |
| Garlic clove, crushed | 1 |

---

## Setup

1. Light the kamado and stabilise at **130°C** — this will take 20–30 minutes. Don't rush it.
2. Fit the **heat deflector**. You want indirect heat only.
3. Add **1–2 chunks of wood** (cherry or oak work well with lamb) directly on the coals before the deflector goes in.

---

## Cook

1. Place the lamb fat-side up on the grate.
2. Insert a probe thermometer into the thickest part, avoiding the bone.
3. Leave it alone for the first **90 minutes**.
4. After 90 minutes, **spritz every 45 minutes** with the mop.
5. Around **75°C internal**, the stall will hit — the temperature will plateau, sometimes for an hour. Don't panic and don't increase the heat.
6. Pull the lamb when it hits **93–95°C** internal and the bone wiggles freely.

---

## Rest & Pull

1. Wrap tightly in **two layers of butcher's paper** (or foil if that's what you have).
2. Wrap that in a towel and rest in a cool box for **45 minutes minimum**.
3. Pull using two forks or your hands. It should fall apart without much effort.

---

## Serve

Works well in:
- **Flatbreads** with yoghurt, cucumber, fresh mint, and a squeeze of lemon
- **Pitta** with harissa and pickled red onion
- **Bowl** over rice or bulgur with pomegranate and herbs

---

## Notes

- A 1kg half leg will have less mass to buffer temperature swings — keep a close eye on your kamado vents to hold 130°C steady.
- If the bark looks like it's getting too dark before you hit 75°C, wrap in butcher's paper and continue. Unwrap for the last 20–30 minutes if you want to firm it back up.
- Lamb leg has enough fat to stay moist at these temps — don't be tempted to go hotter to speed things up.
- Leftovers reheat well with a splash of stock in a covered pan.
