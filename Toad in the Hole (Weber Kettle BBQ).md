# Toad in the Hole (Weber Kettle BBQ)

**Serves:** 4  
**Cook time:** 30 minutes  
**Prep time:** 15 minutes (plus 30 minutes batter rest)

---

## Ingredients

### Batter
- 150g plain flour
- 3 large eggs
- 150ml whole milk
- Pinch of salt

### Everything else
- 8 good quality pork sausages
- 2 tbsp vegetable oil or lard

---

## Equipment
- Cast iron skillet or heavy ovenproof dish
- Weber kettle with full chimney of charcoal
- Dome thermometer

---

## Method

1. **Make the batter** — whisk flour, eggs, milk, and salt together until smooth. Rest for at least 30 minutes.

2. **Set up the BBQ** — light a full chimney of charcoal. Once ashed over, bank coals evenly around the perimeter (volcano method), leaving the centre clear. Open both vents fully.

3. **Preheat the skillet** — place your cast iron skillet in the centre of the grate. Put the lid on and let it heat for 10 minutes. You want 220°C+ at the dome.

4. **Brown the sausages** — place sausages directly over the coals for 3–4 minutes to colour them. Move them back to the skillet.

5. **Add the fat** — add oil or lard to the skillet and let it get smoking hot. **Reminder: Ensure the oil is smoking hot before pouring in the batter to ensure a good rise.**

6. **Pour in the batter** — working quickly, pour the rested batter around the sausages. Put the lid on immediately.

7. **Cook** — do not lift the lid for at least 20 minutes. Cook for 25–30 minutes total until the batter is risen, golden, and has stopped sizzling aggressively.

---

## Notes
 

- The batter rest is non-negotiable — it relaxes the gluten and gives a better rise. Hour rest is better and overnight in fridge is maybe best
- Fat must be smoking hot before the batter goes in. If it isn't, you'll get a dense pancake.
- The setup is key — coals around the perimeter create convection heat that mimics an oven.
- Serve immediately with onion gravy and mustard.
